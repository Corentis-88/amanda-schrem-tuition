Summer maths

Summer maths catch-up before September.

Summer can be a useful time to rebuild maths confidence, especially when a child has started to believe they are "not a maths person". Amanda helps learners slow the subject down, revisit missing foundations and practise methods in a calmer way.

For children moving from Year 6 to Year 7, maths tutoring can focus on number confidence, times tables, fractions, written methods, problem solving and the independence needed for secondary school.

For KS3 learners, Amanda can look for the gaps that make newer topics feel confusing. Sometimes the issue is not the current topic; it is an earlier idea that never became secure.

For Year 10 pupils, mock results can be used carefully. Amanda can help identify common errors, missing knowledge and exam habits to work on before Year 11. This is not a grade guarantee. It is a calmer way to understand what needs attention next.

How Amanda teaches maths

Mistakes become useful information, not proof the child cannot do it.

Maths anxiety often grows when children miss a small step, feel embarrassed, then start avoiding the subject. Amanda uses clear modelling, concrete examples, visual support, vocabulary and repeated practice so methods become familiar and less frightening.

  • Useful for primary maths confidence.
  • Useful for KS2 and early KS3 gaps.
  • Useful for children who rush, freeze or give up quickly.
  • Useful where confidence, language or working memory affects maths.
  • Fully DBS Checked, with secondary classroom experience and a calm approach to confidence in the classroom.
